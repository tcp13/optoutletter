Sending Instructions

Print the opt out letter and mail it to whoever won't leave you alone. Address the envelope to the company's name, with "ATTN: Consumer Privacy Compliance" on the second line, followed by the corporate mailing address (which can typically be found by searching online). If they've sent you physical mail, you can respond to the provided return address or even send it through any prepaid return envelopes.

What should I do after sending an opt out letter?

Keep a record of who you sent an opt out letter to, along with the date you sent it. Some companies may respond to your request within a few weeks, and others may remove you from marketing lists without any response. But remember, just because you've sent an opt out letter doesn't guarantee the company will actually comply with your request! So while you're at it, contact your elected representatives about the need for comprehensive federal privacy regulations.

Why am I prompted to add personal info?

Well this is awkward… a website dedicated to privacy asks for your personal info?! It's important that your opt out letter includes your info so the recipient knows where to stop contacting you. But no worries - this website doesn't save any of your information while generating the opt out letter. If you're extra paranoid, you can use the “Copy Text to Clipboard” button to export your letter to another program (such as Microsoft Word) and add your personal info there.

What other resources can I use to protect my privacy?

  • Opt out of credit and insurance offers at OptOutPrescreen.com
  • Opt out of commercial mail from the Direct Marketing Association, a trade organization for marketers, at DMAchoice.org (there's a $2 service fee)
  • Opt out of telemarketing by adding your phone number to the FTC's National Do Not Call Registry at DoNotCall.gov
  • Refuse delivery of junk mail by marking “Refused - Return to Sender” and leaving the envelope in your mailbox. This usually only works if there's a return address. It won't work for bulk business mail that's been sent as “EDDM,” “ECRWSS,” or “ECRWSH,” which is Direct Mail inserted by the mail carrier to every address on a particular route.
